Lilie Course Capability Map

Eight abilities run through the Lilie curriculum. Rather than mapping what each course teaches in isolation, this document traces how each ability is introduced, practiced, and deepened across the full course sequence — and what students come to believe along the way.

This is a conversation starter for Kyle, Nick, and the academic team — not a finished product. Items marked with (?) are best guesses that need verification.

Structure inspired by the Stanford d.school's shift from teaching "the design process" to teaching transferable design abilities.

The Abilities

Learn from Others

Empathize with unfamiliar people and contexts. Conduct interviews. Observe. Learn from founders, customers, stakeholders, and industry experts.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Intro to Design (220) — First experience interviewing strangers and doing fieldwork; learn to empathize with people unlike themselves DBS (365) — Test prototypes with real early users in the field; collect and analyze feedback data NVC (469) — Customer development with real market stakeholders; coaching accountability
Disco (221) — Conduct informational interviews with industry stakeholders to gather real data New Enterprises (369) — Primary market research with real customers; understand user and customer needs SVF (465) — Assess founding teams and evaluate ventures from the investor perspective
Founder's Journey (360) — Ask thoughtful questions of founders about their journey; learn from lived experience TPDD (222) — Customer interviews, empathy maps, user personas, journey maps

Synthesize and Frame

Make sense of messy information — qualitative and quantitative. Find insights, identify opportunities, build frameworks.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Intro to Design (220) — Synthesize messy qualitative data into insights and opportunities New Enterprises (369) — Construct a value proposition from research; translate assumptions into testable hypotheses Entrepreneurial Strategy (463) — Analyze case studies and develop integrated strategy recommendations
Disco (221) — Evaluate multiple industries and map stakeholders and value creation processes DBS (365) — Analyze field data to decide whether to persevere, pivot, or stop SVF (465) — Construct investment thesis; write investment memo with market analysis, valuation, risks
TPDD (222) — Apply JTBD framework; prioritize features using effort vs. impact analysis Financing (461) — Build data-driven TAM/SAM/SOM model

Experiment and Validate

Test assumptions quickly. Generate and iterate on ideas — written, drawn, or built. Let doing lead thinking.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Intro to Design (220) — Rapidly generate and test ideas; build prototypes at the right fidelity level New Enterprises (369) — Communicate and test value without building (pretotyping); systematically eliminate risk NVC (469) — Go-to-market experiments; translate ideas into measurable traction
DBS (365) — Sprint-based experiments with real users; propose next iteration based on learnings BME (223) — Formulate iterative financial models; track assumptions against reality
TPDD (222) — Build prototypes and run user tests

Build and Ship

Make real things and get them to real people. Move from theory to practice.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Intro to Design (220) — Build prototypes; communicate ideas through artifacts DBS (365) — Build and test low-fidelity prototypes with real users; demo working prototypes; use AI tools critically in the build process NVC (469) — Self-directed startup execution; tangible market progress
New Enterprises (369) — Deliver executive summary and final presentation for a venture concept
TPDD (222) — Build a prototype and run user tests; write PRD and product roadmap

Communicate and Persuade

Tell stories. Pitch ideas. Write memos. Present to investors, customers, teammates, and strangers.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Intro to Design (220) — Communicate stories, ideas, and reflections to diverse audiences Communications (361) — Present in business contexts; network professionally; write emails; run meetings; manage conflicts Financing (461) — Craft executive summary and pitch deck; tell a compelling founder story
Disco (221) — Articulate unique combination of skills, interests, and passion DBS (365) — Demo working prototypes and articulate what was learned Entrepreneurial Strategy (463) — Create personal pitch deck; present strategy recommendations
Founder's Journey (360) — Articulate what surprised them and how it changed their thinking New Enterprises (369) — Deliver executive summary and final presentation SVF (465) — Deliver formal investment recommendation to Investment Committee
BME (223) — Present findings to stakeholders (investors, cofounders, advisors)

Model and Measure

Create financial models. Define metrics. Use data to make decisions and track progress.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
DBS (365) — Select and implement a metric aligned to a value creation hypothesis BME (223) — Build bottoms-up cash flow model; identify assumptions and risks; iterate with increasing resolution Financing (461) — Build TAM/SAM/SOM model; evaluate term-sheet economics; develop fundraising strategy
New Enterprises (369) — Systematically prioritize and eliminate risk through evidence SVF (465) — Apply VC valuation methods; perform structured due diligence
NVC (469) — Present progress with data from prior experiments

Navigate Ambiguity

Persist in the discomfort of not knowing. Find a way forward without clear instructions.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Intro to Design (220) — First encounter with open-ended, ambiguous challenges; no single right answer DBS (365) — Sprint-based work where the next step isn't given; must decide what to build, test, and learn NVC (469) — Self-directed venture execution; structured whitespace with coaching, not answers
Disco (221) — Explore industries without knowing where it leads; map stepping stones from uncertainty New Enterprises (369) — Evaluate whether a problem is worth solving; translate ambiguity into testable hypotheses Entrepreneurial Strategy (463) — Navigate founder dilemmas with no clear right answer; ethical tradeoffs
BME (223) — Interpret data and constraints in ambiguous circumstances; "all models are wrong, but some are useful"

Self-Direct

Own your path. Set goals. Make strategic choices about what to pursue and how.

Introduced Practiced Deepened
Disco (221) — Map stepping stones from where they are to where they want to go; produce a portfolio of opportunities DBS (365) — Propose next experiment based on learnings; decide persevere, pivot, or stop NVC (469) — Set venture milestones and execute in 2-week sprints; self-direct startup execution
Founder's Journey (360) — Reflect on entrepreneurial potential and readiness Entrepreneurial Strategy (463) — Make strategic choices: customer, capabilities, competition, team SVF (465) — Source companies; construct investment thesis; make real investment decisions
Communications (361) — Use communication tools to facilitate internship, career exploration, or job search

How to Read This Map

The Abilities are organized from most broadly shared across the curriculum to most specialized. "Introduced" means students encounter the ability for the first time. "Practiced" means they apply it with increasing independence. "Deepened" means it becomes professional-grade or self-directed.

The Beliefs are grouped by theme rather than by course, to show the cumulative worldview the Lilie curriculum builds. Course names in parentheses indicate where each belief is most strongly developed.

Items marked (?) are best guesses to be corrected and filled in.

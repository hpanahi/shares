Engineering Students in the Entrepreneurship Minor

Prepared for: Entrepreneurship Minor Steering Committee
Data: Lilie enrollment rosters, Fall 2022 – Spring 2026 (11 semesters)
Date: February 2026

Engineering students make up about 37% of all Lilie course enrollment. But over the last three years, exactly 3 engineering students per year have graduated with the minor — flat — while total graduates grew from 9 to 23. Engineering’s share of minor graduates has dropped from 33% to 13%.

This analysis looks at 372 engineering students who took at least one BUSI course between Fall 2022 and Spring 2026.

Graduation gap

The program is growing, but engineering isn’t keeping up. Business graduates went from 3 to 14; engineering stayed at 3.

Where engineering students are in the pipeline

Of 372 engineering students, 10 have completed the minor. 214 (58%) are “exploring” — they took electives but never started a core course. 68% took only one course and stopped.

BUSI 463 is the most common missing course

8 students have 3 of the 4 core courses done. Half of them are missing BUSI 463, which is only offered in Spring at a fixed time. Among current students with 3/4 core, 3 of 6 need 463. This supports the scheduling concern.

Founders Journey and Disco are the entry points

The most common first Lilie course for engineering students is Founders Journey (BUSI 360, 1 credit) at 29%, followed by Disco (BUSI 221) at 22%. These are electives — they don’t count toward the 4 core courses. Only 15% start with Intro to Design and Innovation (BUSI 220).

Of the 263 students who started with an elective, only 48 (18%) ever went on to take a core course. Most try one low-commitment course and don’t continue.

Which engineering students take Lilie courses?

Computer Science (BA + BSCS) makes up 41% of engineering students in Lilie courses. Mechanical Engineering is next at 18%. Most students start as freshmen (44%), but students who start as juniors or later almost never finish the minor (1% completion vs. 3% for early starters).

Enrollment is growing

Unique engineering students in Lilie courses went from 79 to 128 over four academic years. But graduation stayed flat at 3 per year. More engineering students are trying Lilie courses; the issue is what happens after.

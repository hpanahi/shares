MGMT 641 Enrollment Analysis

The Impact of Schedule Changes on Course Enrollment

Kyle,

I wanted to share some data about MGMT 641 that reveals an interesting pattern about scheduling and student needs at Rice Business.

The Pattern

MGMT 641 traditionally served Evening MBA students with a 6:15 PM start time. When the course moved to afternoon slots, enrollment dropped significantly.

The Critical Shift

Looking at Spring 2025 tells the whole story:

Section 1 (2:15 PM start): 18 students enrolled Section 2 (Evening/Weekend): 38 students enrolled

Same course. Same semester. Different times. The evening section has more than double the enrollment.

Timeline of Changes

Before 2022: Course consistently met Tuesday evenings at 6:15 PM - Served Evening MBA students who work full-time - Steady enrollment between 24-38 students - Single 3-hour evening block worked well for working professionals

Spring 2022: Moved to 4:00 PM start - Initial enrollment of 54 (possibly pent-up demand from COVID) - Still a single 3-hour block, but at an awkward time

Spring 2023: Reality sets in - Enrollment drops to 25 students - 4:00 PM doesn’t work for students who leave work at 5:00 PM - Too early for evening students, too late for full-time students

Spring 2025: The experiment continues - Section 1 (Afternoon, 2:15 PM): Only 18 students - Critical issue: This spans TWO elective blocks (2:15-3:45 PM and 4:00-5:30 PM) - Full-time MBAs must sacrifice two elective slots for one course - Section 2 (Evening/Weekend hybrid): 38 students (back to historical levels) - T/Th 8:30-10:00 PM + Saturday 8:00 AM-12:30 PM - Serves working professionals who need evening options

Spring 2026: Pattern continues - Section 1 (Afternoon, Tuesday 2:15-5:30 PM): Enrollment TBD - Same scheduling conflict across two elective blocks - Section 2 (Evening/Weekend hybrid): Enrollment TBD - T/Th 8:30-10:00 PM + Saturday 8:00 AM-12:30 PM - Both sections listed as “Taught In Person Only” but Section 2 functions as hybrid schedule

What This Tells Us

The data suggests MGMT 641 primarily serves working professionals in the Evening MBA program. These students need classes that start after standard work hours.

Moving the course to afternoon slots created a mismatch between the course schedule and its core audience. The 4:00 PM “compromise” time didn’t work for either Full-time or Evening MBA students - too late for daytime students, too early for working professionals.

The 2:15-5:30 PM slot creates an additional barrier for Full-time MBAs: it spans two elective blocks, forcing students to use two elective slots for a single course. This scheduling conflict alone would discourage Full-time MBA enrollment.

The Spring 2025 natural experiment confirms this. When given a choice, students overwhelmingly prefer the evening option. The afternoon section struggles to fill while the evening section approaches capacity.

Implications

This pattern might extend beyond just MGMT 641. As Rice Business considers course scheduling, understanding which courses serve which student populations becomes critical. Evening MBA students need evening options. Moving their courses to afternoon slots risks enrollment and potentially student satisfaction.

The 3-hour block format itself isn’t the issue - both successful and struggling sections use the same 3-hour format. The time of day makes the difference.

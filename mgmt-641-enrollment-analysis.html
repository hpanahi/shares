MGMT 641 Enrollment Analysis

How Course Scheduling Impacts Different MBA Programs

Kyle,

I wanted to share data about MGMT 641 that reveals how schedule changes have impacted enrollment across Rice’s different MBA programs.

Key Finding

MGMT 641 shifted from serving Professional MBA students (evening format) to attempting to serve Full-time MBA students (daytime format). The data shows this shift has been unsuccessful, with daytime enrollment at historic lows while hybrid sections thrive.

The Data Tells a Clear Story

Full-time MBA (2025-2026) Daytime (2:15-5:30 PM) 18 students ❌ Weak
Hybrid (2025-2026) Evening + Weekend 38 students ✅ Strong

Why Full-time MBA Format Fails

  1. Scheduling Conflict: The 2:15-5:30 PM slot spans two elective periods

    • Block 1: 2:15-3:45 PM
    • Block 2: 4:00-5:30 PM
    • Students must sacrifice two elective slots for one course

  2. Limited Demand: Full-time MBA students may have less interest in entrepreneurial strategy compared to working professionals who can immediately apply concepts

  3. Competition: Full-time students have many elective options; this awkward timing makes MGMT 641 less attractive

Spring 2025: The Natural Experiment

Same course. Same semester. Different formats: - Full-time MBA section: 18 students (40% capacity) - Hybrid section: 38 students (84% capacity)

The hybrid section has more than double the enrollment, proving that MGMT 641 resonates most with working professionals who need evening/weekend options.

Implications

  1. MGMT 641 is fundamentally a Professional MBA course - working professionals value and fill these sections

  2. Full-time MBA scheduling doesn’t work - the daytime section struggles despite being the only option for full-time students

  3. The 2022-2024 “transition period” pleased no one - 4:00 PM was too early for professionals, too late for full-time students

  4. Program fit matters more than time format - it’s not just about 3-hour blocks; it’s about serving the right student population at the right time

Recommendation

The data strongly suggests MGMT 641 should return to its roots as a Professional MBA evening course, with a potential hybrid evening/weekend option. The attempt to serve Full-time MBA students with daytime scheduling has resulted in the lowest enrollment in the course’s recent history.

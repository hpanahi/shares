Course Enrollment × Co-Curricular Engagement

Data: Lilie enrollment rosters (Fall 2022 – Spring 2026) + Airtable co-curricular records (events, Launchpad, ventures)
Date: February 2026
N: 873 students enrolled in at least one Lilie course

25% of students who take a Lilie course also engage co-curricularly — attending events, joining Launchpad, or founding a venture. But the rate varies dramatically by course: from 14.8% (Bias and Medical Device Design) to 92.3% (Design Build Ship).

This analysis cross-references enrollment data across 11 semesters with Airtable co-curricular records to map where course-taking and participation overlap.

One in four Lilie students also engages outside the classroom

Of 873 students, 220 (25%) have at least one co-curricular touchpoint. The breakdown: 15% attended events, 19% are venture founders, and 13% joined Launchpad. Most engaged students participate in just one type; only 67 students (8%) do all three.

Build courses have 3–4× the co-curricular engagement of entry courses

Design Build Ship leads at 92.3% engagement, followed by New Venture Challenge at 83.0%. At the other end, entry-level courses like Founder’s Journey (28.0%), Discovery (28.4%), and Intro to Design (32.2%) sit near the baseline. The pattern is clear: courses that involve building and launching ventures attract — or create — students who also engage outside class.

Events, Launchpad, and ventures follow the same pattern

All three engagement types track together across courses. Design Build Ship dominates all three: 69.2% events, 69.2% Launchpad, 84.6% ventures. Venture founding is generally the most common engagement type for upper-level courses, while events attendance is the entry point for earlier courses.

Students who take more courses are far more likely to engage

The relationship between course-taking and co-curricular engagement is steep. Students who take just one course have a 15% engagement rate. By five or more courses, it’s 60%. This doesn’t prove causation — students who are more engaged may simply take more courses — but the gradient is striking.

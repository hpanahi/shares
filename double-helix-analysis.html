Course Enrollment × Co-Curricular Engagement

Data: Lilie enrollment rosters (Fall 2022 – Spring 2026) + Airtable co-curricular records (events, Launchpad)
Date: February 2026
N: 873 students enrolled in at least one Lilie course

20% of students who take a Lilie course also engage co-curricularly — attending events or joining Launchpad. But the rate swings from 11.1% (Bias and Medical Device Design) to 84.6% (Design Build Ship).

This analysis matches enrollment data across 11 semesters against Airtable co-curricular records to see where course-taking and participation overlap.

One in five Lilie students also engages outside the classroom

Of 873 students, 174 (20%) show up in at least one co-curricular record. 15% attended events, 13% joined Launchpad, and 71 students (8%) did both.

Build courses have 3–4× the co-curricular engagement of entry courses

Design Build Ship leads at 84.6%, followed by New Venture Challenge at 73.6%. Founder’s Journey (23.6%), Discovery (21.8%), and Bias & Medical Device Design (11.1%) sit well below baseline. The build-and-launch courses are where co-curricular engagement concentrates — whether those courses attract engaged students or produce them is a separate question.

Events and Launchpad follow the same pattern

Events and Launchpad track together across courses. Design Build Ship leads both: 69.2% events, 69.2% Launchpad. Events are more common in entry-level courses; Launchpad shows up where students already have ventures.

Students who take more courses are far more likely to engage

Students who take one course have a 12% engagement rate. By five or more courses, it’s 51%. This doesn’t prove causation — more-engaged students probably take more courses — but the slope is hard to ignore.

ABET Framing Strategy for Entrepreneurship Capstone

The Three-Step Framing Strategy

Most entrepreneurship capstone work already meets ABET criteria. The gap is usually in how it’s documented, not what students actually do.

Step 1: Translate entrepreneurship language into ABET language

Entrepreneurship Activity ABET-Compliant Framing
Customer discovery Requirements elicitation
Pivot Iterative design refinement
Market analysis Feasibility study (economic + marketability constraints)
Business model canvas System architecture / design rationale
MVP / prototype Proof of concept / functional prototype
Pitch deck Design review presentation
User testing Validation and verification
A/B testing Experimental design and data analysis

Step 2: Every project needs a technical core. The business model wraps around the engineering, not the other way around. Students have to actually design and build something — not just write a business plan.

Step 3: Name your standards and constraints explicitly. Teams often do the engineering work but forget to write down which standards they followed. If the project uses RESTful APIs, cite the spec. If it considers user safety, name the code.

ABET Student Outcomes Map to Entrepreneurship Activities

ABET Outcome Entrepreneurship Activity
(1) Identify, formulate, solve complex problems Customer discovery to find the problem; technical design to solve it
(2) Apply engineering design (health, safety, welfare, global/cultural/social/environmental/economic) User-centered design; market-aware design; regulatory compliance
(3) Communicate effectively Pitches; customer interviews; technical writing
(4) Recognize ethical and professional responsibilities IP, privacy, safety, responsible innovation
(5) Function effectively on a team Startup team dynamics; working across disciplines
(6) Develop and conduct experiments, analyze data MVP testing; A/B testing; user research; market validation
(7) Acquire and apply new knowledge Learning lean startup methods; picking up domain knowledge on the fly
